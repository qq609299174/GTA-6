
The project concept is built around a central town that players rebuild, manage, defend, and expand. During the day, players gather resources, construct buildings, assign workers, trade goods, research threats, and prepare defenses. At night, supernatural forces attack the town from multiple directions. Players may defend the town, repair infrastructure, run logistics, operate buildings, or leave the walls to complete high-risk objectives that affect the invasion.

The long-term direction combines:

- Cooperative town defense inspired by classic Warcraft III RPG defense maps
- Dense horde combat and build pressure inspired by Brotato-style enemy waves
- A living building system where structures produce, consume, upgrade, fail, and affect combat
- Exploration, quests, dungeons, trade routes, and long-term RPG progression
- High-difficulty synchronized cooperative scenarios in selected dungeons and story encounters
- A dark fantasy Tang-dynasty setting involving ancient seals, Qin tombs, terracotta soldiers, corrupted waterways, border towns, caravans, and supernatural threats hidden beneath imperial prosperity

## Core Fantasy

Players are not only leveling up individual characters. They are building and protecting a living town. The town grows from a ruined frontier outpost into a strategic defense point that holds back ancient supernatural forces beneath the Tang empire.

## Design Pillars

1. **The town is a living RPG character**  
   Buildings are not decoration. They have production, staffing, upkeep, active abilities, failure consequences, and long-term progression.

2. **Every role matters**  
   Combat, building, trade, logistics, research, healing, exploration, and defense should all meaningfully affect survival and progression.

3. **Defense is more than killing enemies**  
   Players must manage gates, buildings, civilians, corruption, rituals, repairs, supplies, and outside objectives under pressure.

4. **Exploration changes the defense game**  
   Leaving town to complete quests, destroy enemy sources, rescue NPCs, or recover blueprints can reduce future attacks or unlock new systems.

5. **High-difficulty cooperation is real cooperation**  
   Selected scenarios should require timing, communication, execution, role division, and recovery under combat pressure rather than simple button puzzles.

## Current Status

This repository currently contains design documentation only. It is intended to become the planning base for a playable prototype.

## First Prototype Target

The first playable prototype should prove the following loop:

> Build a small town, prepare defenses, survive night attacks, complete one outside objective, and unlock one meaningful building upgrade.

The first implementation should stay small and focus on verifying whether the core loop is fun before expanding into larger world, story, or multiplayer systems.
